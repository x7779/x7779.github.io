About

A bit about me

Background, research interests, and what drives me.

Hello! I'm Xiaowen Qi, a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Maryland, College Park, advised by Dr. Shuvra S. Bhattacharyya. I obtain a B.S. in Electrical Engineering from the University of Maryland with Summa Cum Laude honors.

My research sits at the intersection of edge and embedded ML, geospatial AI, and Explainable AI (XAI). I build systems that are not just accurate, but transparent, efficient, and deployable — from FPGA-accelerated real-time monitoring pipelines to interpretable graph neural networks for urban crime forecasting.

I also enjoy bridging theory and practice: shipping research into usable tools like PyQt GUIs and hardware-accelerated deployments on FPGAs.

Disentangled STGNN for Urban Crime Prediction

A novel white-box Disentangled Spatial-Temporal GNN (DSTGNN) with residual-guided Graph Structure Learning. Learns dynamic latent topologies beyond known spatial and contextual priors, and uses disentangled channel message passing to independently route semantic signals — achieving both high predictive accuracy on imbalanced crime data and unprecedented edge-level interpretability.

02
Edge ML / FPGA
Ongoing

Real-time Spatter Prediction in Additive Manufacturing

A staged 2D+1D convolutional model that decomposes spatio-temporal video processing to meet stringent FPGA deployment constraints in AM control loops. A multi-scale temporal difference module explicitly encodes motion at three scales, achieving accuracy comparable to full 3D CNNs at significantly lower latency and memory cost.

Paper (draft) ↗GitHub ↗
03
Geospatial AI
PyQt

Geospatial Crime Prediction Platform

An end-to-end crime prediction platform with a parallel-branch model that fuses static geospatial context with temporal crime history. Delivered as multiple PyQt GUIs for one-click data alignment, prediction, interactive mapping, and report generation.
